<p>Welcome to the April 2026 edition of the Fabric Influencers Spotlight, a recurring monthly post here to shine a bright light on the places on the internet where Microsoft MVPs &amp; Fabric Super Users are doing some amazing work on all aspects of Microsoft Fabric.</p>

<p>The Microsoft Fabric Community team has created the&nbsp;Fabric Influencers Spotlight&nbsp;to highlight and amplify blog posts, videos, presentations, and other content related to Microsoft Fabric. We’ve cultivated submissions from members of Microsoft MVPs &amp; Fabric Super Users from the Fabric community that cover the Fabric Platform, Data Engineering &amp; Data Science in Fabric, Data Warehousing, Power BI, Real-Time Intelligence, Data Integration, Fabric Administration &amp; Governance and Databases.&nbsp;</p>

<p>Microsoft Most Valuable Professionals, or MVPs,&nbsp;are technology experts who utilize their deep knowledge of Microsoft products and services to bring together diverse platforms, products, and solutions, to solve real world problems, and to bring “leading edge” content to the data community. </p>

<p>To learn about the Microsoft MVP Award and to find MVPs, visit the&nbsp;<a href="https://mvp.microsoft.com/" rel="noopener" target="_blank">official website</a>.</p>

<p>Fabric Super Users&nbsp;are the heroes of the Fabric Community.&nbsp;They contribute helpful answers, write informative blog posts, post data stories and are key contributors to the Fabric product via engagement with the product group. </p>

<p>To learn more about the Super User program, visit the <a href="https://aka.ms/SuperUsers" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">official website</a>.</p>

<h2>Power BI</h2>

<h3>Cengizhan Arslan, <a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/cengizhanarslan/" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">LinkedIn</a></h3>

<p>Affiliation: Super User</p>

<p>Blog: <a href="https://cengizhan-arslan.medium.com/custom-totals-vs-dax-wrappers-convenience-meets-the-engine-c3f3fef28173" rel="noreferrer noopener" target="_blank">Custom Totals vs DAX Wrappers: Convenience Meets the Engine</a></p>

<p>Power BI's new Custom Totals feature lets you override visual totals without writing DAX, but does the convenience come at a performance cost? This benchmark compares the Custom Totals approach against the traditional AVERAGEX wrapper pattern across two fundamentally different measure types, revealing why the right answer is not the same for additive and non-additive aggregations. The results will surprise anyone who assumed the two approaches were interchangeable.</p>
